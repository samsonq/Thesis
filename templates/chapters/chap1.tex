\chapter{Introduction}

Derivatives pricing has been a significant field in modern finance as investment managers seek to understand the factors that impact derivative contracts and the underlying, and make trading and hedging decisions on those insights. The goal of this research is to explore methodologies in derivatives pricing and hedging strategies, and to propose a data-driven approach using multi-agent systems to optimize financial objectives.

\section{Background of Derivatives Pricing Methods}

Many derivatives pricing models have been created to measure the intrinsic value of derivatives based on factors such as the underlying asset volatility, expiration time, and so on. A notable framework for options pricing known as the Black-Scholes-Merton (BSM) model, developed by Fischer Black, Myron Scholes, and Robert Merton, won the Nobel Prize.
